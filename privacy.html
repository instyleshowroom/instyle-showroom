A. What are Cookies?

The Site uses cookie technology. Cookies are small text files that are downloaded by the browser onto the device used by the User (computer, tablet, smartphone) when they visit a website. Cookies are then retransmitted to the original site during the browsing session or upon any subsequent visit by the User.

There are several types of cookies. Some are strictly necessary for a website to function properly (technical cookies), while others optimize its performance to provide a better user experience or allow you to collect statistics on site usage ( analytical cookies) or to display personalized advertising (profiling cookies).

Our cookies are anonymous and cannot be linked to your personal data. First party cookies are associated with the domain name of the page you are visiting and allow us to identify you by cross-referencing with our database. Your identity is never directly included in the cookie and cannot be intercepted.

Each time you access the Site, regardless of whether a cookie is present or not, we record your browser type, operating system and host along with your source URL, as well as information about the page you requested. This data may then be used in aggregate and anonymous form to perform statistical analysis of website usage.

B. Cookies Used on the Site

The list of technical, analytical and profiling cookies used on the Site is shown below:

1. Technical cookies

Technical cookies are necessary to allow safe and efficient exploration as well as proper functioning of the Site and to improve the User experience. They also allow us to distinguish between the different Users connected to the Site both for security reasons and in order to provide a service requested by the User. No consent is required for these cookies. Technical cookies can be:

  • “session” type, that ensure normal exploration and use of the Site (e.g. for authentication to access secure areas). They are not used for further purposes and are normally installed directly by the owner or operator of the website (so-called "proprietary cookies").

  • “persistent” type that remain stored on the computer's hard drive until their typical expiry or deletion by users/visitors. With the use of persistent cookies, visitors that access the Site (or any other Users who use the same computer) are automatically recognised upon each visit. Persistent cookies satisfy many features in the interest of surfers (e.g. language preferences). Visitors can set the computer's browser so that it accepts/refuses all the cookies or display a warning each time a cookie is offered in order to assess whether or not to accept it. The User is however entitled to modify the default configuration and to disable cookies (i.e. to block them definitively) by setting the highest level of protection.

These type of cookies can also be:

  • “first party”” when they are managed directly by the owner and/or operator of the Site (in this case by the Controller);

  • “third party” when they are generated and managed by third-party providers outside the Site. This Site is unable to control over these subjects and their cookies and to access the information they collect. Responsibility for this type of cookie lies directly and exclusively with the third party itself. Information about the use of these cookies and their purpose, as well as on how to disable them, is provided directly by the third party.

E. How Do I Manage Cookies?

You can manage cookies by changing the preferences of your browser. You can accept all cookies, only some cookies, or decline all cookies. If you decide to opt out of all cookies (including technical cookies), you may not be able to access some areas of the Site or use the services provided.

F. How Do I Disable Cookies?

Each browser allows you to opt out of cookies. Below is some purely indicative information about the procedure to follow in order to disable cookies, depending on the browser used.

Internet Explorer 11.0:

  • Select the Tools button, and then select Internet options.
  • Select the Privacy tab, and under Settings, select Advanced and choose whether you want to accept, block, or be prompted for first-party and third-party cookies.

Firefox 4.0+:

  • Click the menu button and choose Preferences.
  • Select the Privacy & Security panel and go to the Cookies and Site Data section.
  • Select Block cookies and site data and then use the drop-down menu next to Type blocked to choose the types of cookies to block.

Chrome:

  • At the top right, click More and then Settings.
  • At the bottom, click Advanced.
  • Under "Privacy and security," click Content settings and then Cookies.
  • Turn Allow sites to save and read cookie data on or off.

Safari 5.0:

  • Click the Safari menu and select Preferences
  • Click the Security tab.
  • In the Accept cookies section, specify whether you want to accept cookies at all times, never or only from the sites you visit. For an explanation of the different options, click the Help button in the form of a question mark. If Safari is set to block cookies, you may need to accept them temporarily to open the page. Then repeat the steps above and select Always. When you are done using the help, disable the cookies again and delete them.

For more information about other commonly used browsers and how to manage cookie, please refer to: www.allaboutcookies.org.
